# Service in [Location] Landing Page Template

This template provides the structure and content patterns for creating new service-location landing pages.

## Field Guidance

### heroHeadline
**Pattern:** `"[Service] in [Location] that [primary commercial outcome]."`

**Examples:**
- "SEO in Manchester that actually brings in leads, not just rankings."
- "Web design in Leeds that makes your site behave like a sales asset."
- "Digital marketing in Birmingham for teams who want pipeline, not noise."

### heroSubheadline
**Pattern:** `"We work with [location]-based [business type] to [what you actually do], with tracking and reporting that shows exactly what you're getting for your spend."`

**Examples:**
- "Avorria is a performance-focused SEO partner for Manchester businesses. We sort your site, content and tracking so you can see – in hard numbers – what organic search is doing for enquiries and revenue."
- "We design and build websites for Leeds businesses that look sharp and convert visitors into enquiries, bookings and orders – with tracking to prove it."

### problemBullets (5 bullets)

Focus on local pain points:

1. **[Local pain: tried agencies/freelancers, no clear results]**
   - Example: "You've used 'SEO guys' before and all you got was a monthly report and no real change in enquiries."

2. **[Ranking for wrong things or no local visibility]**
   - Example: "You rank for your brand name and a few random terms, but not the searches that matter in [Location] and [Region]."

3. **[Site/SEO/ads disconnected]**
   - Example: "Your site looks okay, but people don't fill in forms or pick up the phone."

4. **[Spending money elsewhere because basics aren't sorted]**
   - Example: "You're spending on ads or directories because SEO has never really been sorted."

5. **[No clear link between channel and revenue]**
   - Example: "Nobody can show you a clear line from SEO to your pipeline."

### solutionBullets (5 bullets)

1. **Location-focused strategy around highest-value services and areas**
   - Example: "Local and regional SEO strategy around the services and areas that actually make you money."

2. **Technical/on-page work specific to service**
   - Example: "Technical and on-page clean-up so Google understands what you do and who you do it for."

3. **Conversion-focused pages with local proof**
   - Example: "Service and location pages built for conversion – clear offers, proof and CTAs."

4. **Tracking and visibility**
   - Example: "Tracking wired into calls, forms and enquiries so you can see what came from where."

5. **Simple, accessible reporting**
   - Example: "Simple dashboards showing organic leads and pipeline – not just 'visibility'."

### keyMetrics (3 metrics)

Provide percentage lifts, timeframes, and context:

1. **Primary metric: % increase in key outcome**
   ```
   {
     value: "+63%",
     label: "Qualified Enquiries",
     description: "Increase in qualified enquiries in 3 months after fixing structure and key pages"
   }
   ```

2. **Secondary metric: Traffic or efficiency gain**
   ```
   {
     value: "+82%",
     label: "Organic Traffic",
     description: "Non-branded organic traffic up over 9 months for a regional service firm"
   }
   ```

3. **Qualitative win or clarity metric**
   ```
   {
     value: "Clear View",
     label: "Local Insights",
     description: "Clear view of which [Location] and [Region] searches actually drive business"
   }
   ```

### testimonialSnippet

**Pattern:** Short win story tied to locality or service outcome

**Structure:**
```
{
  quote: "[We had X problem in Location/service]. Once Avorria [what was done], [specific outcome].",
  author: "",
  role: "",
  company: ""
}
```

**Example:**
"We'd tried a couple of SEO suppliers in Sheffield and got nowhere. Once Avorria rebuilt the structure and pages, our organic leads finally started to look like the reports."

### faqList (4-5 Q&A pairs)

Focus on:

1. **Local fit question**
   - Q: "Do we have to be based in [Location]?"
   - A: Address regional flexibility while emphasizing local market knowledge

2. **Collaboration with existing teams**
   - Q: "Can you work alongside our existing web developer / in-house team?"
   - A: Explain implementation flexibility

3. **Timeline question**
   - Q: "How long until we see results locally?"
   - A: Set realistic expectations (typically 2-3 months early movement, 4-6 months meaningful impact)

4. **Scope/focus question**
   - Q: "Is this just about Google rankings / Do you only do full rebuilds?"
   - A: Clarify commercial focus, not vanity metrics

5. **Fit/budget question (optional)**
   - Q: "Are you only for big budgets / Do you lock us into long contracts?"
   - A: Clarify ideal client profile and contract terms

### targetKeyword
**Pattern:** `"[service] [location]"`

**Examples:**
- "SEO agency Sheffield"
- "web design Leeds"
- "digital marketing agency Yorkshire"

### metaTitle (60 chars max)
**Pattern:** `"[Service] [Location] | [Key Differentiator] | Avorria"`

**Examples:**
- "SEO Agency Sheffield | Performance-Focused SEO | Avorria"
- "Web Design Leeds | Conversion-Focused Websites | Avorria"

### metaDescription (160 chars max)
**Pattern:** `"[Location] [service] that [primary outcome]. [Key differentiators - 2-3 word phrases]."`

**Examples:**
- "Sheffield SEO agency that brings in leads, not just rankings. Technical SEO, content strategy and tracking that shows real business impact."
- "Leeds web design that makes your site behave like a sales asset. Sharp design that converts visitors into enquiries and orders."

## Usage

When creating a new service-location landing page:

1. Replace all `[Service]`, `[Location]`, and `[Region]` placeholders with actual values
2. Adapt problem bullets to reflect local market realities
3. Ensure solution bullets match the specific service offering
4. Use real metrics when available, or adapt placeholder patterns
5. Write testimonials that sound authentic to local market and service type
6. Keep FAQs focused on common objections and practical concerns

## Notes

- Always tie messaging back to commercial outcomes (leads, pipeline, revenue)
- Use plain English, not agency jargon
- Emphasize clarity, tracking, and accountability
- Position as strategic partner, not vendor
- Keep tone confident but pragmatic, not salesy
